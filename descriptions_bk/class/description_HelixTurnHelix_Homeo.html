Superclass: Helix-turn-helix domains

Transcription factors belonging to the HTH domain superclass recognize specific DNA sequences by inserting a "recognition" alpha-helix into the major groove. This helix is followed by a turn and, in most cases, two additional alpha-helices arranged almost perpendicularly to it. HTH DNA-binding domains (DBDs) often include an extra structural element, usually a loop, that projects basic residues into the minor groove, further enhancing sequence specificity.

Visualization of plant TF DBDs in the presence or absence of DNA.

Experimentally resolved structures are depicted in pale cyan for the first monomer and green cyan for the second monomer. The side chains of residues involved in DNA interaction are represented as sticks. DNA appears in orange when co-crystallized with the TF DBD, while it is shown in gray when derived from a different experimentally determined TF/DBD structure used for superimposition.

Class: Homeo domain factors

In the case of HD transcription factors, their diversity in plants arises from the range of adjacent domains, such as the leucine zipper in HD-Zip, which influences DNA binding by enabling dimerization [16]. The 3D structures of plant HD DNA-binding domains have been determined for WUSCHEL (WOX family) (6RYI), revealing a canonical HD fold. Compared to the Engrailed HD protein of Drosophila melanogaster [17], these structures exhibit extended loop regions connecting the three alpha-helices.
