Superclass: Beta-barrel DNA binding domains

Transcription factors (TFs) in this superclass bind DNA using a beta-barrel structure composed of a variable number of beta-strands. In mammals, this superclass is represented by a single family: the Dbp family from the Cold-Shock Domain Factors class. Distant homologs of this family in Arabidopsis (CSP1 and CSP3) function as RNA-binding chaperones but do not exhibit known DNA-binding activity. As highlighted by Wingender, plant-specific B3 domain TFs possess sequence-specific DNA-binding properties and belong to this superclass. The B3 domain consists of approximately 110 residues, forming a structure with seven beta-strands and two alpha-helices arranged into a pseudo-barrel. The loops between beta-strands 1–2 and 4–5 interact with bases in the DNA major groove, enabling each B3 family to recognize specific sequences.

B3 domain transcription factors (TFs) are grouped into four families: LAV (LEAFY COTYLEDON2 (LEC2)-ABSCISIC ACID INSENSITIVE-3 (ABI3)-VAL), ABI/VP1-related protein (RAV), REPRODUCTIVE MERISTEM (REM), and AUXIN RESPONSE FACTOR (ARF). ARFs and LAVs contain a single B3 domain, whereas REM family proteins can possess between one and eleven B3 domains.
