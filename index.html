Plant-TFClass: a structural clasification for plant transcription factors

Blanc-Mathieu, Romain et al. Trends in Plant Science, Volume 29, Issue 1, 40 - 51

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tplants.2023.06.023

Transcription factors (TFs) are proteins that bind DNA specifically via a DNA-binding domain (DBD). TF DBDs fold into various 3D structures that contact DNA through different elements (alpha helices, beta-strands, and loops). The vast diversity of DBD folds in mammalian TF families has been organized hierarchically into superclasses and classes, based on their 3D structure and their mode of interaction with DNA. Such a classification has been missing for plants whose 56 recognized TF families harbor diverse types of DBD, many of which are absent in mammals. Plant-TFClass organize plant TF families into a higher-rank classification.
