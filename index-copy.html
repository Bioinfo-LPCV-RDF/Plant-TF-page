Structural classification for plant transcription factors

Blanc-Mathieu, Romain et al. Trends in Plant Science, Volume 29, Issue 1, 40 - 51

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tplants.2023.06.023

Transcription factors (TFs) bind to specific DNA sequences to regulate gene expression. This fundamental process relies on their DNA-binding domain (DBD). In mammals, the diversity of DBD structural conformations and their interactions with DNA have been used to establish the hierarchical TFClass classification. However, this classification did not account for the numerous DBD types found in plants but absent from mammalian genomes.

We reviewed the 3D structures and models of plant TF DBDs to classify most of the 56 recognized plant TF types within the TFClass framework. This extended classification introduces eight new classes and 37 new families corresponding to DBD structures not found in mammals. Plant-TFClass thus provides a unique resource for comparing TFs across families and organisms.
