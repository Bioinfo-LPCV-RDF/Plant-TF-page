Superclass: Alpha-helices exposed by beta-structures

Members of this superclass feature an all-alpha-helical DNA-binding domain (DBD), but unlike HTH domains, basic domains, and other all-alpha-helical DNA-binding domain superclasses, their DNA-binding helices are exposed by a scaffold of beta-strands. These helices do not insert into either DNA groove; instead, they pack directly against the DNA double helix.

This superclass comprises two classes:

  • SAND domain factors
  • MADS box factors

SAND domain factors are represented by ULTRAPETALA genes in plants and VARL genes in green algae. However, sequence-specific DNA binding activity has never been demonstrated for these factors [src] [src].
