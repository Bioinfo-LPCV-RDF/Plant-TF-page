Superclass: Beta Barrel DBDs



Class: B3

As highlighted by Wingender [10], plant-specific B3 domain transcription factors (TFs) exhibit sequence-specific DNA binding and belong to this superclass. The B3 domain comprises approximately 110 residues, featuring seven beta-strands and two alpha-helices folded into a pseudo-barrel structure. Residues in the loops between beta-strands 1–2 and 4–5 directly contact DNA bases in the major groove, enabling each B3 family to recognize distinct sequences [66, 67, 68].

B3 domain TFs are categorized into four families:

  • LAV (LEAFY COTYLEDON2 (LEC2)-ABSCISIC ACID INSENSITIVE-3 (ABI3)-VAL)
  • RAV (ABI/VP1-related protein)
  • REM (REPRODUCTIVE MERISTEM)
  • ARF (AUXIN RESPONSE FACTOR) [69]

ARF and LAV families contain a single B3 domain, while REM family proteins can possess between one and 11 B3 domains [70]. RAV proteins uniquely feature an additional AP2/ERF DNA-binding domain alongside the B3 domain. Most ARF TFs also include a PB1 oligomerization domain [71, 72, 73] and often bind DNA as dimers, with preferences for specific orientations and spacing of ARF binding sites [54, 74, 75, 76]. Unlike other families, studies on VRN1 [77] and REM16 [78] within the REM family suggest that these TFs do not exhibit sequence-specific DNA binding.
