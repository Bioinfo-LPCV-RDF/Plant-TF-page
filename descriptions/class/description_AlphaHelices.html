Superclass: Alpha-helices exposed by beta-structures

Members of this superclass have an all-alpha-helically folded DBD but – contrary to the HTH domains, the basic domains, and the other all-alpha-helical DNA-binding domain superclasses – the DNA-binding helices are exposed by a scaffold of beta-strands and do not insert in either DNA groove but are packed against the DNA double helix.

This superclass contains two classes: the SAND domain factors and the MADS box factors
