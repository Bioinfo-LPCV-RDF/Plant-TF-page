Superclass: Yet-Undefined DBDs

Class: PLATZ

The PLATZ transcription factors (TFs), specific to Archaeplastida, are zinc-dependent DNA-binding proteins. Their zinc finger coordination motif features a unique N-terminal consensus signature: C-x₂-H-x₁₁-C-x₂-C-x₄₋₅-C-x₂-C-x₃₋₇-H-x₂-H, distinguishing it from other known zinc-binding motifs. Additionally, the motif includes four conserved cysteine residues in its central region [src].

Structural models of PLATZ proteins reveal a variety of folds, including alpha-helices, a beta-hairpin, and a beta-sheet. However, in the absence of experimental structures in complex with DNA or extensive modeling, it remains uncertain whether this family should be definitively classified within the zinc-coordinating DNA-binding domain (DBD) superclass.
