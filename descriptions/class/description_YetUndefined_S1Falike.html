Superclass: Yet-Undefined DBDs

This superclass includes transcription factors (TFs) with functionally well-characterized DNA-binding domains (DBDs), but which await structural data for definitive classification. In TFClass, it comprises four established classes, plus an ‘uncharacterized’ class that groups six families. Among these, plants possess NFX1-like proteins, whose TF activity has yet to be firmly established [82].

We have expanded this superclass to include six additional classes, corresponding to plant TF families with well-documented activity but lacking critical structural insights into their DBD/DNA interactions. For three of these classes—cysteine-rich polycomb-like proteins (CPP) [83], DNA-binding protein phosphatases (DBP) [84], and S1Fa-like factors [85]—there is no structural data, and AlphaFold2 predictions of their 3D DBD structures are uninformative (either low-confidence models or no matches in 3D structure comparisons).

For the remaining three classes—GRAS, PLATZ, and BBR/BCP—putative classification is discussed later, based on experimental or modeled structural data.
