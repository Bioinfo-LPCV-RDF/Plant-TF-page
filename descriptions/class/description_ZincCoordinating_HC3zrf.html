Superclass: Zinc-Coordinating DNA-Binding domain

Class: HC3 zinc ribbon finger

The ALOG family of proteins, proposed as a novel plant-specific transcription factor family [src], has been functionally characterized in several angiosperms and Marchantia polymorpha. Recent structural analysis (PDB ID: 8P5Q) [src] has provided key insights into their molecular architecture. These proteins are believed to have evolved from a retrotransposon recombinase, where a zinc ribbon was inserted into a core tetrahelical structure [src].

Structural studies of the DNA-binding domain (DBD) in complex with DNA reveal a bundle of alpha-helices, with an HC3-motif zinc ribbon playing a critical role. This zinc ribbon helps position the alpha-helices and the loop that directly interacts with DNA. Given the presence of the HC3 motif in the zinc ribbon, we established a new class—HC3 zinc ribbon factors—within the zinc-coordinating DNA-binding proteins superclass to accommodate the ALOG family.
